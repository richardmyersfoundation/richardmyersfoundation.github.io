About

    Mr. Richard Myers was an activist of the Lockport/Fairmont community for over 75 years. He retired from Caterpillar, Inc after 42 years of service where he served as the Machinist union steward for 19 years. In addition, Mr. Myers served as the elected President, Vice President and Board Member of the Fairmont School board District 89 for a span of 17 years. Mr. Myers worked diligently to support the young boys in the neighborhood who were without a father-figure and living in single parent homes; providing additional support to help navigate around the crime, drugs and unforeseen activities that distracted many from getting a solid education.

    Mr. Myers commitment in holding clothing and food drives, a host of free haircuts for neighborhood young men and an after school, Books Before Ball tutoring services, were all exemplary. However, his deep commitment with his own personal funds supporting various students with basic needs, fighting to make a difference is commendable. His heart ached over the conditions many of the students lived in and he fought alongside other community members to make changes in the life one student at a time.

    Mr. Myers was an avid sports fan and coach dedicating his time with various student-athletes. To address the academic issues many faced, he helped develop an infrastructure that provided transportation from school to the RMF center.
